The Problem

Wealth management runs on twelve disconnected tools.

A CRM for relationships. A separate system for portfolio data. Another for compliance logging. A fourth for client reporting. None of them talk to each other, so an advisor's day gets spent copying numbers between screens instead of reading the filing that actually moves a position. The tools didn't get smarter as the data got bigger — they just multiplied. Every hour lost to re-entry, reconciliation, and manual monitoring is an hour not spent with a client.

One operating system, eight layers of automation.

Every layer runs independently and communicates through a shared agent architecture — so nothing has to be re-entered twice.

Portfolio Intelligence

Continuously scores drift, concentration, and risk across every client account, not just the ones flagged manually.

Workflow Automation

Data Connectors

Client Portal

Compliance Engine

Multi-Agent Architecture

Specialized agents for research, risk, and compliance coordinate on a shared context — no manual handoffs.

Automations

From client data to a compliant action, in seven steps.

The same pipeline behind a research summary, a rebalance alert, or a quarterly review deck.

01

Ingest

Pull filings, prices, and account data from custodians and market feeds the moment they update.

02

Analyze

Agents parse the data against each client's mandate, risk profile, and existing positions.

03

Draft

A summary, alert, or recommendation gets written in plain language, sourced back to the filing.

04

Check

The compliance engine screens the draft against firm policy and regulatory rules before it moves.

05

Approve

The advisor reviews and signs off in one click — never blind, never blocking.

06

Deliver

The output reaches the client portal, the CRM, and the audit log at the same time.

07

Monitor

The agent keeps watching that position, ready to trigger the next cycle without being asked.

Security & Compliance

Compliance that runs continuously, not once a quarter.

Trust isn't a feature you bolt on to an advisory platform — it's the reason one exists.

01

Audit Trails

Every agent action, recommendation, and edit is logged and timestamped automatically.

02

Access Controls

Role-based permissions down to the account level, enforced at the data layer.

03

Data Encryption

Client and portfolio data encrypted in transit and at rest, by default.

04

Regulatory Alignment

Built against SEBI's advisory framework from the schema up, not retrofitted later.

05

Anomaly Detection

Agents flag unusual account activity the moment it deviates from a client's pattern.

06

Human-in-the-Loop

No recommendation reaches a client without an advisor's explicit sign-off.
